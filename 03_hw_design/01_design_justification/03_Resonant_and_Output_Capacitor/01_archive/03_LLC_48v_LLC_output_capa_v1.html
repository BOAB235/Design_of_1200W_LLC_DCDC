Output capacitor Co¶

Formulas¶

For a resistive load, the output capacitor is determined by $$ C_o \geq C_{o_{min}} = \frac{I_o}{8 \cdot f_{sw} \cdot \Delta V_{out}} $$

Where:

  • $C_o$ : Output capacitance (Farads, F)
  • $I_o$ : Output load current (Amperes, A)
  • $f_{sw}$ : Switching frequency of the converter (Hertz, Hz)
  • $\Delta V_{out}$ : Maximum allowed output voltage ripple (Volts, V)
    $$ESR_{max} = \frac{\Delta V_{out}}{I_{rect\_peak}} = \frac{\Delta V_{out}}{\frac{\pi}{4} \cdot I_o\cdot{2}} $$ $$I_{C_o} = I_o \sqrt{\frac{\pi^2}{8} - 1}$$

Where:

  • $I_{C_o}$ : RMS current of the capacitor @ f_sw = f_nom

Numerical Implementation of the Formulas¶

We must use fsw_min to the worst case

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Io} &= 25.000 \; \;\textrm{(A)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{fsw}_{min} &= 60170.000 \; \;\textrm{(Hz)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{DV} &= 0.250 \; \;\textrm{(vpp)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Co}_{min_{uF}} &= 1 \times 10 ^ {6} \cdot \frac{ \mathrm{Io} }{ 8 \cdot \mathrm{fsw}_{min} \cdot \mathrm{DV} } = 1 \times 10 ^ {6} \cdot \frac{ 25.000 }{ 8 \cdot 60170.000 \cdot 0.250 } &= 207.745 \; \;\textrm{(uF)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{ESR}_{max_{m}} &= 1 \times 10 ^ {3} \cdot \frac{ \mathrm{DV} }{ \mathrm{Io} \cdot \frac{ \pi }{ 2 } } = 1 \times 10 ^ {3} \cdot \frac{ 0.250 }{ 25.000 \cdot \frac{ 3.142 }{ 2 } } &= 6.366 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] I_{Co} &= \mathrm{Io} \cdot \sqrt { \frac{ \left( \pi \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 8 } - 1 } = 25.000 \cdot \sqrt { \frac{ \left( 3.142 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 8 } - 1 } &= 12.086 \; \;\textrm{(Arms)} \end{aligned} $

First proposition¶

B40910A8127M000 aluminum electrolytic capacitors with Temp_max = 150C, Below a screenshoot of the datasheet [2].

Figure 1: VRMS

Let's start with a configuration of 5 capacitors in parallel

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Nb}_{capa} &= 5 \; \\[10pt] C_{capa_{nom}} &= 120 \; \;\textrm{(uF)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Margin} &= 20 \; \;\textrm{(\%)} \\[10pt] C_{capa} &= C_{capa_{nom}} \cdot \left( 1 - \frac{ \mathrm{Margin} }{ 100 } \right) = 120 \cdot \left( 1 - \frac{ 20 }{ 100 } \right) &= 96.000 \; \;\textrm{(uF, Worst case)} \\[10pt] C_{eq} &= C_{capa} \cdot \mathrm{Nb}_{capa} = 96.000 \cdot 5 &= 480.000 \; \;\textrm{(uF > 208uF ok)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{ESR}_{capa} &= 17 \; \;\textrm{( @ 100kHz)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{ESR}_{eq} &= \frac{ \mathrm{ESR}_{capa} }{ \mathrm{Nb}_{capa} } = \frac{ 17 }{ 5 } &= 3.400 \; \;\textrm{( < 6.36m OK)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Icapa}_{max} &= 4.600 \; \\[10pt] \mathrm{Ieq}_{max} &= \mathrm{Icapa}_{max} \cdot \mathrm{Nb}_{capa} = 4.600 \cdot 5 &= 23.000 \; \;\textrm{(@ Arms @ 125C 100kHz > 12.08Arms ok)} \end{aligned} $

Voltage ripples

$ \begin{aligned} \Delta_{V_{out}} &= \frac{ \mathrm{Io} }{ 8 \cdot \mathrm{fsw}_{min} \cdot C_{eq} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-6} } = \frac{ 25.000 }{ 8 \cdot 60170.000 \cdot 480.000 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-6} } &= 0.108 \end{aligned} $

Self heating¶

Power Dissipation of Each Capacitor

$ \begin{aligned} I_{each_{capa}} &= \frac{ I_{Co} }{ \mathrm{Nb}_{capa} } = \frac{ 12.086 }{ 5 } &= 2.417 \\[10pt] P_{selfHeating} &= \mathrm{ESR}_{capa} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-3} \cdot \left( I_{each_{capa}} \right) ^{ 2 } \\&= 17 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-3} \cdot \left( 2.417 \right) ^{ 2 } \\&= 0.099 \; \;\textrm{(W)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $

Estimation of Thermal Resistance Rth

$ \begin{aligned} \Delta_{T} &= 150 - 125 &= 25 \; \;\textrm{(C)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{ESR} &= 0.017 \; \\[10pt] I &= 4.600 \; \;\textrm{(Arms)} \\[10pt] P_{dissip} &= \mathrm{ESR} \cdot \left( I \right) ^{ 2 } = 0.017 \cdot \left( 4.600 \right) ^{ 2 } &= 0.360 \; \;\textrm{(W)} \\[10pt] R_{th} &= \frac{ \Delta_{T} }{ P_{dissip} } = \frac{ 25 }{ 0.360 } &= 69.498 \; \;\textrm{(C/W)} \end{aligned} $

The self heating estimation and the max ambiant temp

$ \begin{aligned} \Delta_{T} &= P_{selfHeating} \cdot R_{th} = 0.099 \cdot 69.498 &= 6.903 \; \;\textrm{(C => low delta temp)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Margin} &= 30 \; \;\textrm{(C)} \\[10pt] T_{max} &= 150 \; \\[10pt] T_{amb_{max}} &= T_{max} - \Delta_{T} - \mathrm{Margin} = 150 - 6.903 - 30 &= 113.097 \; \;\textrm{(C)} \end{aligned} $

Voltage margin

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Vo}_{max} &= 54.000 \; \;\textrm{(VDC)} \\[10pt] V_{max_{datasheet}} &= 63 \; \;\textrm{(VDC)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Voltage}_{Margin} &= 100 \cdot \frac{ V_{max_{datasheet}} - \mathrm{Vo}_{max} }{ V_{max_{datasheet}} } = 100 \cdot \frac{ 63 - 54.000 }{ 63 } &= 14.286 \; \;\textrm{(\%)} \end{aligned} $

This solution is acceptable, but the voltage margin is limited.

Self heating¶

Power Dissipation of Each Capacitor

$ \begin{aligned} I_{each_{capa}} &= \frac{ I_{Co} }{ \mathrm{Nb}_{capa} } = \frac{ 12.086 }{ 10 } &= 1.209 \\[10pt] P_{selfHeating} &= \mathrm{ESR}_{capa} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-3} \cdot \left( I_{each_{capa}} \right) ^{ 2 } \\&= 59 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-3} \cdot \left( 1.209 \right) ^{ 2 } \\&= 0.086 \; \;\textrm{(W)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $

Estimation of Thermal Resistance Rth

$ \begin{aligned} \Delta_{T} &= 150 - 125 &= 25 \; \;\textrm{(C)} \\[10pt] P_{dissip} &= \mathrm{ESR}_{capa} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-3} \cdot \left( \mathrm{Icapa}_{max} \right) ^{ 2 } \\&= 59 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-3} \cdot \left( 2.300 \right) ^{ 2 } \\&= 0.312 \; \;\textrm{(W)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{th} &= \frac{ \Delta_{T} }{ P_{dissip} } = \frac{ 25 }{ 0.312 } &= 80.100 \; \;\textrm{(C/W)} \end{aligned} $

The self heating estimation and the max ambiant temp

$ \begin{aligned} \Delta_{T} &= P_{selfHeating} \cdot R_{th} = 0.086 \cdot 80.100 &= 6.903 \; \;\textrm{(C => low delta temp)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Margin} &= 30 \; \;\textrm{(C)} \\[10pt] T_{max} &= 150 \; \\[10pt] T_{amb_{max}} &= T_{max} - \Delta_{T} - \mathrm{Margin} = 150 - 6.903 - 30 &= 113.097 \; \;\textrm{(C)} \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Vo}_{max} &= 54.000 \; \;\textrm{(VDC)} \\[10pt] V_{max_{datasheet}} &= 100 \; \;\textrm{(VDC)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Voltage}_{Margin} &= 100 \cdot \frac{ V_{max_{datasheet}} - \mathrm{Vo}_{max} }{ V_{max_{datasheet}} } = 100 \cdot \frac{ 100 - 54.000 }{ 100 } &= 46.000 \; \;\textrm{(\%)} \end{aligned} $

Resonanat capacitor Cr¶

Chosing a capacitor for Cr¶

The inputs data

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Cr}_{nF} &= 116.209 \; \;\textrm{(nF)} \\[10pt] V_{In_{max}} &= 400.000 \; \;\textrm{(V)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{fsw}_{min} &= 60170.000 \; \;\textrm{(Hz)} \end{aligned} $

The RMS voltage of the resonant capacitor

$ \begin{aligned} X_{Cr} &= \frac{ 1 }{ 2 \cdot \pi \cdot \mathrm{fsw}_{min} \cdot \mathrm{Cr}_{nF} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-9} } = \frac{ 1 }{ 2 \cdot 3.142 \cdot 60170.000 \cdot 116.209 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-9} } &= 22.761 \; \;\textrm{(Ohm)} \\[10pt] V_{Cr} &= \mathrm{Ir}_{rms} \cdot X_{Cr} = 10.354 \cdot 22.761 &= 235.672 \; \;\textrm{(V)} \\[10pt] V_{Cr_{rms}} &= \sqrt { \left( \frac{ V_{In_{max}} }{ 2 } \right) ^{ 2 } + \left( V_{Cr} \right) ^{ 2 } } = \sqrt { \left( \frac{ 400.000 }{ 2 } \right) ^{ 2 } + \left( 235.672 \right) ^{ 2 } } &= 309.098 \; \;\textrm{(vrms)} \end{aligned} $

The power dissipation of each capacitor

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{ESR}_{u} &= 63.740 \; \;\textrm{()} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Ir}_{rms_{1}} &= 1.294 \; \;\textrm{(Arms)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Ir}_{rms_{max}} &= 2 \; \;\textrm{(Arms)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{PD}_{uW} &= \mathrm{ESR}_{u} \cdot \left( \mathrm{Ir}_{rms_{1}} \right) ^{ 2 } = 63.740 \cdot \left( 1.294 \right) ^{ 2 } &= 106.771 \; \;\textrm{(uW)} \end{aligned} $

The Rth estimation

$ \begin{aligned} T_{max} &= 125 \; \\[10pt] T_{100} &= 100 \; \\[10pt] \Delta_{T} &= T_{max} - T_{100} = 125 - 100 &= 25 \\[10pt] \mathrm{PD}_{uW_{max}} &= \mathrm{ESR}_{u} \cdot \left( \mathrm{Ir}_{rms_{max}} \right) ^{ 2 } = 63.740 \cdot \left( 2 \right) ^{ 2 } &= 254.962 \\[10pt] R_{TH} &= \frac{ \Delta_{T} }{ \mathrm{PD}_{uW_{max}} } = \frac{ 25 }{ 254.962 } &= 0.098 \; \;\textrm{(C/uW)} \end{aligned} $

Each capacitor's self-heating and the maximum permissible ambient temperature.

$ \begin{aligned} \Delta_{T} &= \mathrm{PD}_{uW} \cdot R_{TH} = 106.771 \cdot 0.098 &= 10.469 \\[10pt] T_{amb_{max}} &= 125 - \Delta_{T} = 125 - 10.469 &= 114.531 \; \;\textrm{(C)} \end{aligned} $

Understanding the Derating Curve of the Capacitors¶

One point calculation

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Ta} &= 105 \; \\[10pt] \Delta_{T} &= T_{max} - \mathrm{Ta} = 125 - 105 &= 20 \\[10pt] \mathrm{PD}_{uW_{max}} &= \frac{ \Delta_{T} }{ R_{TH} } = \frac{ 20 }{ 0.098 } &= 203.969 \\[10pt] \mathrm{Ir}_{rms_{max_{Ta}}} &= 1.789 \; \\[10pt] \mathrm{Fa} &= \frac{ \mathrm{Ir}_{rms_{max_{Ta}}} }{ \mathrm{Ir}_{rms_{max}} } = \frac{ 1.789 }{ 2 } &= 0.894 \end{aligned} $

The same calculation is repeated to construct the derating curve.
