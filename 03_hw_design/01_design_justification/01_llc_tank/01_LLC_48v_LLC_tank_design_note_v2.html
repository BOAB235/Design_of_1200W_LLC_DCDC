Voltage Gain – Theoretical Overview¶

Figure 1: The simplified schematic and the equivalent small-signal AC model


The voltage gain function (normalized) expression is:

$$ M_g = \left| \frac{L_n \cdot f_n^2}{\left[(L_n + 1) \cdot f_n^2 - 1\right] + j\left[(f_n^2 - 1) \cdot f_n \cdot Q_e \cdot L_n\right]} \right| $$

With:

$$ L_n = \frac{L_m}{L_r}, \quad Q_e = \frac{\sqrt{\frac{L_r}{C_r}}}{R_e}, \quad f_n = \frac{f_{sw}}{f_0}, \quad f_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{L_r C_r}} $$ See page 3 formula (23) [1].

Transfo ratio and Voltage Gain¶

$ \begin{aligned} n &= \frac{ V_{In_{nom}} }{ \mathrm{Vo}_{nom} \cdot 2 } = \frac{ 380 }{ 48 \cdot 2 } &= 3.958 \end{aligned} $

Choose an integer value to simplify the transformer design.

$ \begin{aligned} n &= \operatorname{round} \left( n \right) = \operatorname{round} \left( 4 \right) &= 4 \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Vf} &= 0.200 \; \;\textrm{(drop voltage in the mos)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{efficiency} &= 0.950 \; \;\textrm{(hypothesis)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{loss} &= 1 - \mathrm{efficiency} = 1 - 0.950 &= 0.050 \\[10pt] \mathrm{Io}_{nom} &= \frac{ \mathrm{Power} }{ \mathrm{Vo}_{nom} } = \frac{ 1200 }{ 48 } &= 25.000 \; \;\textrm{(A)} \\[10pt] V_{loss} &= \frac{ \frac{ \mathrm{Power} \cdot \mathrm{loss} }{ \mathrm{efficiency} } }{ \mathrm{Io}_{nom} } = \frac{ \frac{ 1200 \cdot 0.050 }{ 0.950 } }{ 25.000 } &= 2.526 \; \;\textrm{(v)} \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{margin} &= 0.010 \; \\[10pt] \mathrm{Mg}_{min} &= n \cdot \frac{ \mathrm{Vo}_{min} \cdot \left( 1 - \mathrm{margin} \right) + \mathrm{Vf} }{ \frac{ V_{In_{max}} }{ 2 } } \\&= 4 \cdot \frac{ 42 \cdot \left( 1 - 0.010 \right) + 0.200 }{ \frac{ 400 }{ 2 } } \\&= 0.836 \\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Mg}_{max} &= n \cdot \frac{ \mathrm{Vo}_{max} \cdot \left( 1 + \mathrm{margin} \right) + \mathrm{Vf} + V_{loss} }{ \frac{ V_{In_{min}} }{ 2 } } \\&= 4 \cdot \frac{ 54 \cdot \left( 1 + 0.010 \right) + 0.200 + 2.526 }{ \frac{ 360 }{ 2 } } \\&= 1.273 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{Mg}_{max110} &= \mathrm{Mg}_{max} \cdot \left( \frac{ 110 }{ 100 } \right) \\&= 1.273 \cdot \left( \frac{ 110 }{ 100 } \right) \\&= 1.400 \\[10pt] \end{aligned} $

Lm, Lr, Cr tank¶

Below we will use grid search to find the best values for Ln and Qe.

The idea:

  • Change Ln in the range: start = 1, stop = 10, step = 0.01 (around 100 points)
  • Change Qe in the range: start = 0.1, stop = 1, step = 0.01 (around 10 points)

We will select the Ln and Qe values that give an Mg value closest to Mg_max110.

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Lnc} &= 3 \; \\[10pt] \mathrm{Qec} &= 0.550 \; \end{aligned} $

Lnc = 3 and Qec = 0.55 represent an optimal compromise due to the following:

  • Moderate gain slope (ΔM/Δf):
    In the inductive region, the gain rises gradually with frequency, enabling stable control without abrupt sensitivity shifts.
  • Limited frequency span (f_min to f_max):
    These parameters restrict the switching frequency range, simplifying component design, controller implementation, and consistent ZVS operation.

Equivalent resistor¶

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Re}_{nom} &= 8 \cdot \left( n \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot \frac{ \mathrm{Vo}_{nom} }{ \left( \pi \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot \mathrm{Io}_{nom} } \\&= 8 \cdot \left( 4 \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot \frac{ 48 }{ \left( 3.142 \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot 25.000 } \\&= 24.901 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{Re}_{nom} &= \operatorname{round} \left( \mathrm{Re}_{nom} ,\ 3 \right) \\&= \operatorname{round} \left( 24.901 ,\ 3 \right) \\&= 24.901 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{Re}_{110} &= 8 \cdot \left( n \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot \frac{ \mathrm{Vo}_{nom} }{ \left( \pi \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot \mathrm{Io}_{nom} \cdot 1.1 } \\&= 8 \cdot \left( 4 \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot \frac{ 48 }{ \left( 3.142 \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot 25.000 \cdot 1.1 } \\&= 22.637 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] P_{re_{110}} &= \frac{ \left( \frac{ V_{In_{nom}} }{ 2 } \right) ^{ 2 } }{ \mathrm{Re}_{nom} } \\&= \frac{ \left( \frac{ 380 }{ 2 } \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 24.901 } \\&= 1449.741 \\[10pt] \end{aligned} $

Lm, Lr, Cr values¶

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Cr}_{nF} &= \frac{ 1 \times 10 ^ {9} }{ 2 \cdot \pi \cdot \mathrm{Qec} \cdot f_{nom} \cdot \mathrm{Re}_{nom} } \\&= \frac{ 1 \times 10 ^ {9} }{ 2 \cdot 3.142 \cdot 0.550 \cdot 100000.000 \cdot 24.901 } \\&= 116.209 \; \;\textrm{(nF)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Cr} &= 116.209 \; \\[10pt] \mathrm{Cr} &= \operatorname{round} \left( \mathrm{Cr} ,\ 3 \right) \\&= \operatorname{round} \left( 116.209 ,\ 3 \right) \\&= 116.209 \\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Lr} &= \frac{ 1 }{ \left( 2 \cdot \pi \cdot f_{nom} \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot \mathrm{Cr} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-9} } \\&= \frac{ 1 }{ \left( 2 \cdot 3.142 \cdot 100000.000 \right) ^{ 2 } \cdot 116.209 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-9} } \\&= 0.000 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{Lr}_{uH} &= \mathrm{Lr} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {6} \\&= 0.000 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {6} \\&= 21.797 \; \;\textrm{(uH)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{Lr}_{uH} &= \operatorname{round} \left( \mathrm{Lr}_{uH} ,\ 3 \right) \\&= \operatorname{round} \left( 21.797 ,\ 3 \right) \\&= 21.797 \\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Lm} &= \mathrm{Lr} \cdot \mathrm{Lnc} \\&= 0.000 \cdot 3.000 \\&= 0.000 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{Lm}_{uH} &= \mathrm{Lm} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {6} \\&= 0.000 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {6} \\&= 65.392 \; \;\textrm{(uH)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} L_{second_{uH}} &= \frac{ \mathrm{Lm}_{uH} }{ \left( n \right) ^{ 2 } } \\&= \frac{ 65.392 }{ \left( 4 \right) ^{ 2 } } \\&= 4.087 \; \;\textrm{(uH)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $

Verification

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Qcal} &= \frac{ \sqrt { \frac{ \mathrm{Lr} }{ \mathrm{Cr} \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-9} } } }{ \mathrm{Re}_{110} \cdot 1.1 } = \frac{ \sqrt { \frac{ 0.000 }{ 116.209 \cdot 1 \times 10 ^ {-9} } } }{ 22.637 \cdot 1.1 } &= 0.550 \\[10pt] \mathrm{Qec} &= 0.550 \; \end{aligned} $

Fsw limites and primary secondary currents¶

$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{fsw}_{min} &= \mathrm{fn}_{min} \cdot f_{nom} \\&= 0.602 \cdot 100000.000 \\&= 60170.000 \; \;\textrm{(Hz)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{fsw}_{max} &= \mathrm{fn}_{max} \cdot f_{nom} \\&= 1.562 \cdot 100000.000 \\&= 156220.000 \; \;\textrm{(Hz)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{wmin} &= 2 \cdot \pi \cdot \mathrm{fsw}_{min} \\&= 2 \cdot 3.142 \cdot 60170.000 \\&= 378059.260 \; \;\textrm{(rad/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{wmax} &= 2 \cdot \pi \cdot \mathrm{fsw}_{max} \\&= 2 \cdot 3.142 \cdot 156220.000 \\&= 981559.209 \; \;\textrm{(rad/s)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Im}_{rms} &= 2 \cdot \sqrt { 2 } \cdot n \cdot \frac{ \mathrm{Vo}_{nom} }{ \pi \cdot \mathrm{Lm} \cdot \mathrm{wmin} } \\&= 2 \cdot \sqrt { 2 } \cdot 4 \cdot \frac{ 48 }{ 3.142 \cdot 0.000 \cdot 378059.260 } \\&= 6.992 \; \;\textrm{(Arms)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Io} &= 25.000 \; \;\textrm{(Arms)} \\[10pt] \mathrm{Ioe}_{rms} &= 1.1 \cdot \pi \cdot \frac{ \mathrm{Io} }{ n \cdot 2 \cdot \sqrt { 2 } } \\&= 1.1 \cdot 3.142 \cdot \frac{ 25.000 }{ 4 \cdot 2 \cdot \sqrt { 2 } } \\&= 7.636 \; \;\textrm{(Arms @ 110%)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{Ios}_{rms} &= \mathrm{Ioe}_{rms} \cdot n \\&= 7.636 \cdot 4 \\&= 30.545 \; \;\textrm{(Arms)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Ir}_{rms} &= \sqrt { \left( \mathrm{Im}_{rms} \right) ^{ 2 } + \left( \mathrm{Ioe} \right) ^{ 2 } } \\&= \sqrt { \left( 6.992 \right) ^{ 2 } + \left( 7.636 \right) ^{ 2 } } \\&= 10.354 \; \;\textrm{(Arms)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} $
