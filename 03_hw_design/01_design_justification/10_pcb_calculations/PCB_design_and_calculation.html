PCB alowed current¶

Formula¶

$ I = K \cdot \left( \Delta_{T} \right) ^{ 0.44 } \cdot \left( A \right) ^{ 0.725 } $

Where :

$ \begin{aligned} I \; \;\textrm{(Current (A))} \\ A \; \;\textrm{(Cross section in seq. mils)} \\ \Delta_{T} \; \;\textrm{(Temperature rise in °C)} \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} K &= 0.048 \; \;\textrm{(Outer layer)} \end{aligned} $
$ \begin{aligned} K &= 0.240 \; \;\textrm{(Inner layer)} \end{aligned} $

See PC-2221A(L), page 50

Hypothesis:

  • Differential voltage = 600V (400V with margin)
  • B2 : External conductors, uncoated, over 3050m
$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Spacing}_{Norm} &= 0.005 \; \;\textrm{(mm/V)} \\ \mathrm{Voltage} &= 600 \; \;\textrm{(V)} \\ \mathrm{Spacing} &= \mathrm{Spacing}_{Norm} \cdot \mathrm{Voltage} = 0.005 \cdot 600 &= 3.000 \; \;\textrm{(mm)} \end{aligned} $
